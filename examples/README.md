# Examples

All files here are inputs to, or outputs generated by, `tectonics.py`
v0.3.0-beta against a synthetic self-hosted deployment
(`openai_compatible/llama3.1:8b`). The data is illustrative — no reading here
is an observation of any real endpoint.

## Inputs

- **`manifests/`** — two lmd-spec Coverage Manifests, both validated against
  the [specification](https://github.com/mossrake/lmd-spec). They declare two
  processes consuming the *same* endpoint at different consequence levels
  (an invoice auto-approval at Level 4, a meeting summarizer at Level 2) —
  the N×M situation the consequence join exists for. Point the tool at them
  with `"manifests_dir": "examples/manifests"` or `--manifests
  examples/manifests`.

## Outputs

- **`status.json`** — actual output of `tectonics.py status --json` with the
  manifests above loaded: the latest per-model reading joined with its
  consuming processes. This is the payload to ingest into Grafana, Datadog,
  ServiceNow, or any other dashboard.
- **`evidence.json`** — actual output of `tectonics.py evidence`: drift
  evidence items (prose description + structured `metrics`) conformant to the
  lmd-spec evidence schema, ready to attach to the drift dimension of a
  Coverage Manifest.

Regenerating: these outputs were produced by the tool itself; if a future
version changes an output shape, regenerate rather than hand-edit.
